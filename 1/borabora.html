Discover Bora Bora

A tropical paradise in the South Pacific

About Bora Bora

Bora Bora, located in French Polynesia, is a small South Pacific island known for its turquoise lagoon, white-sand beaches, and luxurious overwater bungalows. Often referred to as the "Pearl of the Pacific," Bora Bora is a dream destination for honeymooners and travelers seeking a tropical escape.

The island is surrounded by a coral reef and a stunning lagoon, offering opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and other water activities. With its lush green mountains and crystal-clear waters, Bora Bora is a true paradise on Earth.

Must-See Attractions

Mount Otemanu

The iconic volcanic peak of Bora Bora, offering breathtaking views and hiking opportunities.

Matira Beach

One of the most beautiful beaches in the world, known for its soft white sand and clear waters.

Coral Gardens

A vibrant underwater world perfect for snorkeling and exploring marine life.

Lagoonarium

A natural aquarium where you can swim with sharks, rays, and tropical fish.

Culture & Traditions

Bora Bora's culture is deeply rooted in Polynesian traditions, offering a unique blend of history, art, and hospitality.

Local Cuisine

Try traditional Polynesian dishes like poisson cru (raw fish marinated in coconut milk), taro, and fresh tropical fruits.

Traditional Crafts

Bora Bora is known for its handmade crafts, including woven baskets, shell jewelry, and tapa cloth.

Festivals

Experience Polynesian culture through events like the Heiva Festival, featuring traditional dance, music, and sports.
