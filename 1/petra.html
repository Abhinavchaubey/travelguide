Discover Petra

The Rose City of Jordan

About Petra

Petra, also known as the "Rose City," is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan. Famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system, Petra is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The city was established as early as the 5th century BC by the Nabataeans, an ancient Arab tribe. It served as a major trading hub and is renowned for its stunning structures carved directly into the red sandstone cliffs.

Must-See Attractions

The Treasury (Al-Khazneh)

The most iconic structure in Petra, the Treasury is a magnificent facade carved into the sandstone cliff, believed to be a mausoleum or temple.

The Monastery (Ad-Deir)

One of the largest monuments in Petra, the Monastery offers breathtaking views after a climb of over 800 steps.

The Siq

A narrow gorge leading to Petra, the Siq is a dramatic entrance with towering cliffs on either side.

Royal Tombs

A collection of impressive tombs carved into the rock face, showcasing the grandeur of Nabataean architecture.

Culture & Traditions

Petra's culture is deeply rooted in the history of the Nabataeans, with influences from Greek, Roman, and Arab traditions.

Local Cuisine

Enjoy traditional Jordanian dishes like mansaf (lamb cooked in yogurt sauce), falafel, and kunafa (a sweet cheese pastry).

Traditional Crafts

Petra is known for its handmade pottery, jewelry, and sand art, often inspired by the city's ancient heritage.

Festivals

Experience Petra's culture through events like the Petra Desert Marathon and the Petra Night Show, where the Treasury is illuminated by candles.
