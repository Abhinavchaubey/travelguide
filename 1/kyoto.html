Discover Kyoto

The cultural heart of Japan

About Kyoto

Kyoto, located in the Kansai region of Japan, is renowned for its rich history, traditional culture, and stunning architecture. Once the capital of Japan for over a thousand years, Kyoto is home to numerous temples, shrines, and gardens that reflect the country's heritage.

From the iconic Fushimi Inari Shrine with its thousands of red torii gates to the serene Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Kyoto offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and modernity.

Must-See Attractions

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Famous for its thousands of red torii gates, this shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Shinto god of rice and prosperity.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

A serene bamboo forest that offers a peaceful escape and stunning photo opportunities.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

A Zen temple covered in gold leaf, reflecting beautifully in the surrounding pond.

Gion District

Kyoto's famous geisha district, known for its traditional wooden machiya houses and tea houses.

Culture & Traditions

Kyoto is the cultural heart of Japan, preserving traditions that date back centuries.

Local Cuisine

Try Kyoto specialties like kaiseki (multi-course meal), yudofu (tofu hot pot), and matcha (green tea) sweets.

Traditional Crafts

Kyoto is famous for its kimono weaving, pottery, and lacquerware. Visit Nishijin for traditional textiles.

Festivals

Experience Kyoto's culture through events like the Gion Matsuri, one of Japan's most famous festivals.
